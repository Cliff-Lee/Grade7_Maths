Research and Explore

Now that you have learned about using scientific notation, it's your turn to apply it! Research a real-world situation where scientific notation is used and create your own calculation. Here are some ideas to get you started:

  • Astronomy: Calculate the distance between planets or stars, or the time it takes for light to travel from the sun to Earth.
  • Physics: Find out how much energy a certain object has (e.g., energy released during an explosion) and express it in standard form.
  • Population Studies: Research the population of different countries or the growth rate of a city and calculate projections using scientific notation.
  • Environmental Science: Investigate the amount of CO2 emissions produced by different industries and express these figures in scientific notation.

Write down your calculation and explain how you used scientific notation to represent the quantities. Be ready to share your findings with the class and discuss why scientific notation is useful in this context.
